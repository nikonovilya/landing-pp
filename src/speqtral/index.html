Kicking off a quantum key distribution in space using Precious Payload software

The company:

SpeQtral is a Singapore‑based quantum-secure communication systems provider. They recently secured $8.3M to fund their work. The company is reinventing cryptography for the quantum age using satellites in LEO to secure data networks across the globe.

Empty

SpeQtral's revolutionary “QKD” technology is a crucial component for quantum‑secure communication networks. QKD uses quantum physics‒as opposed to current math‑based solutions‒to distribute symmetric encryption keys used to secure communication networks.

The situation:

Precious Payload has been acquainted with the SpeQtral team for several years. In 2019 we conducted an interview at the SmallSat conference in Utah to learn more about their quantum-secure communication systems and the team’s first mission, “SpooQy‑1,” for our #knowyouroptions YouTube series about space entrepreneurs. SpooQy‑1 was a tech demo deployed from the ISS to generate polarization-entangled photon pairs and de‑risk future quantum key distribution missions.

Empty

Following the successful in‑orbit demonstration and their latest infusion of capital, the company began to plan a larger 16U mission for early 2024. In November, 2021, they reached out to see how Precious Payload’s digital mission manager could help kickstart this project. SpeQtral wanted us to make a dry run of RFI and tenders, assist with orbital slot reservations, and facilitate the export/import of hardware for their new mission.

The Challenge:

Safety is a major concern for the SpeQtral team. They wanted to ensure their QKD technology wouldn’t be damaged during the handover and integration of the payload. As a Singapore‒registered company, SpeQtral is also restricted to working with Russian and Chinese launch providers.
